Rome, Itlay

Our first stop was Rome, The Eternal City and we stayed for five days. We did all the basic things, including The Vatican, the tour of Ancient Rome (The Coliseum, The Pantheon, The Roman Forum), The Borghese Gallery, and much more.

Florence, Italy

Here we stayed in Tusacany and explored the countryside. We went to differnt wineries, satyed on the side of a mountain, and drove through the mountains. We explored the museums and ate in the sqaure. We saw Michealangelo's David at the Academia and works by Botticelli, da Vinci, and Raffael at the Uffizi. Afterall it is the Birthplace of the Renaissance

Venice, Italy

The City of Water. venivce was interesting because to get anywhere you either had to walk or take a boat. It waas beautiful though, something that you would never see anywhere else. We toured the city by water on the canals and dined at Cafe Florian and Hotel Danieli. We toured Doge's palce, the residence of the former Republic of Venice's ruler Because our time in Venice was the least scheduled out, we used trip advisor and other websites like it to find things to do or places to eat.

The Galeria d'Orsay wehre we admired the works of Van Gogh and Monet. The Palce at Versailles where Louis XVI and Marie Antoinette lived.
